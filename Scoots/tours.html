The Wonders of Cozumel

Cozumel's white-sand beaches slope down into transparent waters that surround the island's spectacular coral reefs. The interior of the island is relatively undeveloped, making it a refuge for endemic and endangered species of birds and other animals.

History of Cozumel

The Caribbean island of Cozumel sits 10 miles (16km) off the eastern coast of the Yucatan Peninsula in southeastern Mexico.

Once a commercial and cerimonial center during the Myan period (250CE-950CE), Cozumel now runs as a popular tourist destination and home to several small archaeological sites. Juan de Grijalva lead the first Spanish expedition to Cozumel in 1518. In 1519 Hernan Cortes used the island as a base for Spanish efforts to conquer the Maya.
